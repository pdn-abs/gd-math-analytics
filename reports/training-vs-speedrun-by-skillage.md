## Insights

### 1. The ~50% training drop is a pure comprehension failure — time is not a factor

Training is untimed, so no user is dropping because they ran out of time. The consistent 43–57% training drop across *every* SkillAge means roughly half of users at every age genuinely cannot complete the untimed content. This is a content design and scaffolding problem, not a time pressure problem. There is no "easy" SkillAge to enter.

### 2. Speedrun drops confirm most users who learn untimed *can* handle timed

For SA2, 3, 5, 8, 9, 10 speedrun drops are 8–24%. These users already understood the mechanics in training; the added time constraint doesn't break them. Speedrun mode works as intended — it filters for fluency on top of comprehension.

### 3. SA6 speedrun (40% drop) is a time-pressure-specific failure

SA6 users pass untimed training at 51% — similar to other SAs. But then 40% of speedrun survivors drop. Since speedrun adds only the time constraint, this means SA6 content specifically is **learnable but not yet fluent** for a large share of users. They can do it slowly (pass training) but not quickly (fail speedrun). SA6 is the only SkillAge where fluency is a wall on top of comprehension.

### 4. SA7 training collapse (20% conversion to speedrun) is a raw comprehension problem

No time limit, yet 47% drop in training and almost none of the completers return for speedrun. This means SA7 content is simply too hard to understand even without any pressure. The 20% conversion to speedrun is the lowest in the entire pool — SA7 needs its training levels redesigned, not just adjusted for timing.

### 5. SA5 is the cleanest funnel after training

Despite having the worst hard-level drops in training (from the prior analysis), users who survive SA5 training almost never drop speedrun (11%). SA5 comprehension is the barrier; once cleared, fluency comes naturally. The fix is entirely in the training content.

### 6. SA2 dominates volume — fixing it has the largest absolute impact

609 SA2 training starters = 55% of the entire 1,107-user pool. Even at SA2, 47% drop in training. Given the volume, fixing SA2 training retention would recover more users in absolute terms than any other single intervention across all SkillAges.
